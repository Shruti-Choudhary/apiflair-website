Our food security faces an invisible crisis

Climate change is accelerating crop diseases faster than we can detect them. The consequences ripple through our entire agricultural ecosystem.

01

The Detection Gap

Crop diseases spread rapidly across large farmlands, often invisible until it's too late. By the time symptoms appear, significant damage is already done.

Traditional visual inspection methods can't keep pace with climate-driven pathogens. Farmers are left guessing, leading to reactive rather than preventive measures.

40% of crops lost annually $220B in trade losses
02

The Pollination Crisis

Commercial bee colonies are collapsing at alarming rates. Hive transport stress and pesticide exposure are making pollination services increasingly unreliable and expensive.

Without stable pollination, entire crop systems face existential risk. The supply chain that feeds billions is breaking down.

30-40% hive losses $634.7M in beekeeper losses

A Destructive Cycle

These aren't separate problems—they're interconnected parts of a collapsing ecosystem.

Crop diseases trigger excessive fumigation. Fumigation harms natural pollinators. Pollinator loss reduces crop health. Weakened crops become more susceptible to disease. The cycle intensifies.

Every season, farmers face impossible choices: protect their crops with chemicals that harm the very insects they need for pollination, or risk losing everything to disease.

What if we could break this cycle?

We're building technology that addresses both challenges simultaneously—offering farmers real-time biosurveillance and reliable pollination services.
